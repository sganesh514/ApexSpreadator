📈 Why Some Symbols/Trades Did Well
1. High Volatility Momentum (High ATR/Beta)

High-momentum leaders like MELI (+$44.9k P&L, 75% WR) and NVDA (+$15.9k P&L, 66% WR) performed exceptionally well. Because their underlying price changes rapidly and clean trend bounces occur off supply/demand zones, the strategy hits the sold-strike take-profit levels quickly before theta or counter-trend movements eat into option prices.

2. Robust Support Retests with Volatility Buffer

Symbols like KDP and OXY benefited from strong zone respect. Retests of demand zones followed by immediate trend continuation allowed the system to maximize option credit/debit expansions with minimal drawdown, capitalizing on the SMA 20-period trend filter alignment.

📉 Why Some Symbols/Trades Did Poorly
1. Consolidations and Zone Breaches (Whipsawing)

Symbols like QCOM (-$4.0k P&L, 0% WR) and DXCM (-$3.6k P&L, 0% WR) experienced prolonged consolidations. When the underlying stock whipsaws and invalidates a supply/demand zone, the options structure is forced to trigger stop losses. Since vertical spreads contain capped but direct risk, multiple zone breaches cause sequential stop-outs.

2. Low Beta Inefficiencies (Underperformance)

Low-volatility symbols like CSCO and COST struggle to reach profit targets within the designated DTE window. Option spreads on these symbols are prone to stagnant price action, leading to time-decay forced exits or stop-outs on minor trend deviations.

🛠️ Actionable Strategy Optimization Plan

Based on quantitative observations, the following system tunings are recommended to optimize performance:

  • Dynamic Position Sizing: Keep the IV-based sizing multiplier active. High volatility reduces position sizes, which successfully limits drawdowns on volatile trades while keeping larger contracts allocated to stable, low-IV setups.
  • Beta Filtering: Introduce a minimum Average True Range (ATR) or Beta threshold to filter out low-volatility assets (e.g., CSCO, COST) that fail to reach targets, as they have low profit contribution and consume capital efficiency.
  • Sector Exposure Cap: Maintain the sector-correlation guardrail limit of 1 position per sector. This successfully shields the portfolio from concurrent stop-loss triggers in correlated groups (e.g., semiconductors) during market-wide swings.
